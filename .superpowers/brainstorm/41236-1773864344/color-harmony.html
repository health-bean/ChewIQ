Color Harmony Analysis

Are Ocean Teal (195°) and Soft Coral (30°) good together? Let's look at the color theory.

The relationship: Split-Complementary

Your two colors sit 165° apart on the color wheel (195° teal → 30° coral). This is a split-complementary harmony — not direct opposites (180°), but close. This is actually ideal because:

Primary
195°
Ocean Teal
Accent
30°
Soft Coral

True complementary (180° apart) creates maximum tension — great for sports brands, bad for a calming health app. Split-complementary gives you contrast and visual interest without the clash. It's the most commonly used harmony in healthcare and wellness design for exactly this reason.

Why this specific pairing works

Teal 600 — Primary actions, brand
Cool, calming, associated with healing water. Dominant color (~80% of UI).
Coral 500 — Accent, warmth, attention
Warm counterpoint. Used sparingly (~10%) for CTAs, warnings, the "IQ" in the logo.
Teal-gray — Text, borders, neutral UI
Desaturated teal for secondary text and borders. Warmer than pure gray (~10%).

The 80/10/10 rule: 80% teal (dominant calm), 10% coral (warm pops), 10% neutral teal-gray. The coral never overwhelms — it's a gentle counterweight that makes the teal feel warmer by contrast. Without it, the teal would feel clinical and cold. Without the teal, the coral would feel like a baby product.

Real-world reference

This is the same harmony used by:

  • Headspace — teal/blue primary + orange accent
  • Calm — blue primary + warm gold accent
  • One Medical — teal primary + coral accent (almost identical)
  • Noom — teal-green primary + warm secondary

It's a proven pattern in health/wellness because the cool-warm balance signals both trust (cool) and care (warm).

Verdict: Yes, these are excellent complementary colors.

Split-complementary at 165° gives you contrast without tension. The low-medium saturation on both colors keeps it easy on the eyes. The 80/10/10 ratio prevents either from dominating. This is not a weird combination — it's the most battle-tested harmony in wellness design, executed with your own specific hues.
