A. Monochrome Teal

One color family for everything. Logo is teal-dark + teal-medium. Buttons, badges, links — all teal at different lightnesses. Semantic colors (red for symptoms, green for allowed) provide natural variety. No accent color needed.

Think: Apple Health, Linear, Notion — one brand color, neutrals do the rest.

B. Teal + Warm Neutrals

Teal is the only chromatic color. But the neutrals are warm — cream backgrounds, warm grays for text and borders (hue 80° instead of pure gray). The warmth comes from the environment, not a competing accent. Teal pops against the warmth.

Think: Aesop, Kinfolk magazine — monochrome brand + warm paper-like surfaces.

C. Teal + Plum

Teal primary + muted plum/mauve accent (hue 320°). These are analogous-cool — both on the cool side of the wheel, so they harmonize naturally without the warm/cool tension. The plum adds richness without fighting the teal. More unexpected, more premium.

Think: Ritual vitamins, modern apothecary — cool sophistication with depth.

💡 My honest recommendation: B (Teal + Warm Neutrals). The problem was never "which accent color" — it was "why do we need two chromatic colors competing?" Option B lets the teal be the hero. The warmth comes from the cream/paper surfaces instead of from a second color. It's the most calming, most premium, and most distinctive approach. The semantic colors (green for allowed, red for symptoms, amber for warnings) already provide all the variety you need.
